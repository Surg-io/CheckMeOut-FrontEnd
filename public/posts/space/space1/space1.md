## What is a Makerspace?
A makerspace is a collaborative workspace designed for creativity, innovation, and hands-on learning. It provides access to tools, technology, and resources that enable individuals to design, prototype, and build projects across various disciplines, including electronics, woodworking, 3D printing, and more.

## Facilities and Equipment
- **3D Printers**: Print custom parts and prototypes using various materials.
  ![3D Printer](images/3D_Printer.jpg)
- **Laser Cutters**: Precision cutting for wood, acrylic, and other materials.
  ![Laser Cutter](images/Laser_Cutter.jpg)
- **Electronics Workstations**: Equipped with soldering irons, oscilloscopes, and microcontrollers.
- **Woodworking Tools**: Saws, drills, and other tools for fabrication.
- **Software and Computers**: Access to CAD, coding, and design software.

## Who Can Use the Makerspace?
The makerspace is open to students, faculty, and community members who want to explore and develop their skills. Training sessions are available for those new to the equipment.

## How to Reserve Equipment
Reservations can be made through our online system, ensuring access to specific tools and workstations at designated times.
